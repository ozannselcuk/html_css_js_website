Fruits

Apples

Apple is one of the most produced and traded temperate fruits. Turkey lies in the West Asia and Europe origin center and are known to have substantial diversity.

Most of the world’s commercially produced apple cultivars belong to Malus domestica. Apples have been grown in Turkey since as early as 6500 BCE and its domestication began from a large and rich gene pool.

Quince

Quince has an important place in Turkish culture and tradition. Trees are found in forests and as wild shrubs. Quince blossom time is recognized as the beginning of summer season and is mentioned in folk songs.

he main species, C. oblanga, is widely grown in Turkey, especially in western Anatolia, for its edible fruits. Quince orchards are established with grafted varieties suitable for fresh consumption. The fruit is consumed as a pome fruit, can be cold-stored and consumed throughout the winter.

Turkey is 1 st. country in the fig production and exportation. The most favourite one is "Bursa Black", which is produced in the Bursa region. Almost all of the figs originated from this region are exported.

Bursa Black, which has the highest quality in the world is demanded at increasing amounts every year by Europen countries because of its extraordinary flavor, firm skin, size, attractive shape, colour, and long shelf time.

Cherry

All cherries, sweet, sour and mahaleb cherry belong to the Cerasus genus. Some of the species and varieties originated from mountains of the Black Sea Region.

The origin of sour cherry is the area between Caspian Sea and north Anatolia mountain ranges. Thus, the Latin name of sour cherry (Prunus cerasus L.) derives from the old name of the city “Kerasus” in the Black Sea Region of Turkey which is today known as Giresun. Kütahya Province is traditionally known to possess a rich population for sour cherry.

Pomegranate

Pomegranate is one of the traditional fruits cultivated for centuries in Turkey which is one of the gene centers of pomegranate diversity.

Wild forms are distributed from Anatolia towards other Mediterranean countries. Many different types and forms all contributed as germplasm for the genetic improvement of the crop. The species is grown in regular orchards sporadically mixed in other fruit trees, and in roadside hedges from the coastal to the mountainous areas.

Orange

Today, oranges rank just behind apples as the most consumed fruit in the world. The majority of the oranges in Turkey are grown in Antalya and Mersin, in the Mediterranean Region.

Turkey accounts for 3.7% of global orange production, ranking it the seventh largest producer and fourth largest exporter. Oranges make up 50% of total citrus fruit production.

Mandarin

Mandarin cultivars are grown in most regions with temperate climates. The fruit of this evergreen, small tree that contains essential oils is a favourite among people in Turkey.

It is grown extensively in the Aegean, Mediterranean and Eastern Black Sea Regions of the country. Tangerines account for 25% of citrus fruit production in Turkey. It is a sweet, fragrant, tasty and vitamin-rich fruit.

Grapefruit

The grapefruit tree is not unlike and orange in the ideal conditions required for its growth. It is a fruit very rich in vitamin C. It may be eaten raw or cut into halves, sprinkled with sugar and put under the grill for three to four minutes.

It is a citrus variety whose homeland is on the shores of South East Asia. Grapefruit, which started to be grown in the USA in the 19th century, spread rapidly all over the world. Generally seeking a mild climate, grapefruit is a fruit grown between 40 degrees north and 40 degrees south latitudes.

Lemon

Lemons are the fruits of evergreen trees whose culture forms are grown in all temperate countries. Citrus lemon in Turkey constitute 20% of the production.

The homeland of the lemon is North India or the south of China. However, it is known that the Mediterranean type lemon with high acidity grew in the borders of today's Iraq around 3000 BC.

Plums

Anatolia is one of the centers of genetic diversity for plums is found as widely dispersed in northern, western, and southern Anatolia is the center of origin.

In Turkey plums thrive everywhere except for the high mountain meadows and the very hot and dry areas of Southeast Anatolia. Many different varieties are grown in Turkey, the most common being red plums, greengages (eaten while crisp and not yet ripe) and prune plums.

Peach

Peach bears edible juicy fruits with various characteristics, most called peaches and others (the glossy-skinned varieties), nectarines.

Turkey accounts for about 3 percent of total world peach production. Theprovinces of Bursa, Canakkale (both in the Marmara region), Izmir (Aegean region) and Mersin (Mediterranean region)are the major growing areas in Turkey.

Nectarine

Peaches and nectarines are the same species, though they are regarded commercially as different fruits.

Nectarine is also referred to as a "shaved peach" or "fuzzless peach", due to its lack of fuzz or short hairs. Though fuzzy peaches and nectarines are regarded commercially as different fruits, with nectarines often erroneously believed to be a crossbreed between peaches and plums, or a "peach with a plum skin", nectarines belong to the same species as peaches.
